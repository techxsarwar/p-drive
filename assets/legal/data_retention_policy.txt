1. Purpose

This Data Retention Policy explains how P-Drive stores, retains, deletes, and manages user information, files, account records, logs, and related data.

The purpose of this policy is to promote transparency regarding how long information may remain within P-Drive systems.

3. General Principles

P-Drive aims to:

Minimize unnecessary data retention

Protect user privacy

Maintain service reliability

Comply with legal obligations

Improve security practices

Data is generally retained only for as long as reasonably necessary.

5. User Files

Uploaded files may remain stored until:

The user deletes the file

The account is deleted

Storage limitations require action

Legal obligations require removal

Technical issues require corrective action

P-Drive does not guarantee indefinite retention of uploaded content.

Users should maintain independent backups of important files.

6. Deleted Files

When a user deletes a file:

The file may not be immediately removed from all systems.

Deletion may involve:

Primary storage removal

Backup removal

Replication cleanup

Cache expiration

Deletion processing times may vary.

Certain residual copies may temporarily remain within backup systems for operational purposes.

7. Account Information Retention

Account information may be retained while:

The account remains active

The user continues using services

Security obligations require retention

Legal obligations require retention

Retention periods may vary depending on operational requirements.

8. Account Deletion

Users may request account deletion.

Following deletion requests:

Account access may be disabled

Personal information may be scheduled for removal

Associated content may be scheduled for deletion

Certain records may be retained where reasonably necessary for:

Legal compliance

Security investigations

Fraud prevention

Dispute resolution

9. Metadata Retention

P-Drive may retain limited metadata including:

File sizes

Upload timestamps

Download timestamps

Storage usage records

Operational statistics

Metadata may be retained longer than file contents when required for legitimate operational purposes.

10. Security Logs

Security-related logs may be retained to:

Detect abuse

Investigate incidents

Improve platform security

Protect users

Maintain service integrity

Retention periods may vary depending on security requirements.

11. Diagnostic and Error Logs

Diagnostic information may be retained for:

Troubleshooting

Performance analysis

Reliability improvements

Bug resolution

Such information may be periodically removed or anonymized.

12. Subscription and Billing Records

Billing records may be retained as reasonably necessary for:

Accounting purposes

Financial audits

Tax compliance

Dispute resolution

Fraud prevention

Retention periods may depend on applicable legal requirements.

13. Backup Systems

P-Drive may maintain backup systems designed to:

Improve reliability

Recover from failures

Protect against accidental loss

Data contained within backup systems may remain available for limited periods following deletion from primary systems.

Backup removal may occur according to operational schedules.

14. Legal Retention Requirements

Certain information may be retained longer when required by:

Applicable law

Court orders

Regulatory obligations

Lawful governmental requests

Such retention may continue beyond ordinary deletion schedules.

15. Data Anonymization

Where appropriate, P-Drive may:

Anonymize information

Aggregate information

Remove identifying elements

Anonymized data may be retained for research, analytics, security, or operational purposes.

16. User Responsibility

Users are responsible for:

Maintaining backups

Downloading important files before deletion

Verifying account information

Managing stored content

P-Drive is not responsible for losses resulting from user-initiated deletion actions.

17. Data Destruction Practices

When data is scheduled for removal, P-Drive may utilize reasonable technical measures intended to:

Remove active records

Eliminate unnecessary copies

Reduce residual retention

Protect deleted information from unauthorized access

Complete removal across all systems may not occur instantly.

18. Retention Limitations

Because of:

Distributed systems

Replication mechanisms

Backup processes

Technical constraints

certain information may temporarily persist beyond deletion requests.

P-Drive seeks to minimize such retention where reasonably possible.

21. Final Statement

P-Drive is committed to responsible data handling practices and privacy-conscious retention policies.

While we strive to minimize retention wherever reasonably possible, certain operational, legal, security, and technical requirements may require information to be retained for limited periods beyond user actions.

Users should always maintain independent backups of important information and should not rely solely upon any single storage provider for long-term preservation of critical data.
